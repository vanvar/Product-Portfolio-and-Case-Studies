# Business Case: AI Closet Organizer

**Version:** 1.0  
**Date:** February 2025

---

## Executive Summary

**Opportunity:** Build an AI-powered closet organization app that solves the "nothing to wear" problem through effortless 10-minute setup and intelligent daily outfit suggestions.

**Financial Impact (Year 1):**
- Revenue: $479K
- Development Cost: $320K
- ROI: 150%
- Payback Period: 8 months

**Recommendation:** APPROVE — Strong product-market fit, defensible AI moat, clear path to profitability.

---

## Market Sizing

### Total Addressable Market (TAM)
- US smartphone users interested in fashion: 180M
- TAM: $8.6B annually ($4.99/month × 12 × 10% adoption)

### Serviceable Available Market (SAM)
- Users who've tried closet/styling apps: 22M
- SAM: $1.3B annually

### Serviceable Obtainable Market (SOM - Year 1)
- Target downloads: 100K
- Premium conversion (8%): 8K subscribers
- SOM: $479K annually

## Revenue Model

### Freemium Pricing

**Free Tier:**
- Up to 50 items
- Basic outfit suggestions (3 daily)
- Ads (banner + occasional interstitial)

**Premium ($4.99/month or $39.99/year):**
- Unlimited items
- Calendar & weather integration
- Advanced AI suggestions
- Underused item insights
- Ad-free
- Cloud backup

## Unit Economics

### Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)
- Blended CAC (organic + paid): $12
- Paid CAC only: $18

### Lifetime Value (LTV)
- Avg subscription duration: 18 months
- Monthly churn: 5.5%
- LTV: $4.99 × 18 = $90
- LTV/CAC: 7.5:1 (excellent)

### Payback Period
- 2.4 months (CAC $12 / MRR $4.99)

## Alternative Approaches Considered

### Option A: Manual Cataloging Only (No AI)
**Pros:** Simpler to build, lower dev cost  
**Cons:** Same onboarding friction as competitors, no differentiation  
**Decision:** Rejected — AI is our core value prop

### Option B: Subscription-Only (No Free Tier)
**Pros:** Higher revenue per user  
**Cons:** Higher barrier to trial, lower conversion  
**Decision:** Rejected — freemium proves value first

### Option C: Ad-Supported Only
**Pros:** Accessible to all users  
**Cons:** Low revenue, poor user experience  
**Decision:** Rejected — premium experience worth paying for
